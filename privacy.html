Privacy Policy

Last Updated: May 20, 2026

At Stacknomic ("we", "our", or "us"), we respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your personal data. This privacy policy will inform you as to how we look after your personal data when you visit our website (regardless of where you visit it from) and tell you about your privacy rights.

1. Information We Collect

We only collect information that is necessary to provide our digital infrastructure services to you. This includes:

  • Identity Data: First name, last name, business name.
  • Contact Data: Business email address, phone number.
  • Technical Data: Industry type (EdTech, FinTech, etc.) as submitted through our contact forms.

2. How We Use Your Data

We use the information collected exclusively for the following business purposes:

  • To respond to your inquiries regarding our web development and infrastructure services.
  • To schedule technical discovery calls and consultations.
  • To draft and send Detailed Project Reports (DPRs), invoices, and contracts.

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third parties for marketing purposes.

3. Client Code & Intellectual Property (IP) Ownership

Stacknomic operates as a custom development agency. A core tenet of our business model is total client ownership. Once a project is fully paid for and deployed:

  • 100% of the Source Code, JSON architectures, and front-end assets are transferred to you.
  • Any student or financial data processed by the applications we build for you remains strictly your property. Stacknomic does not retain, monetize, or access your users' data post-deployment unless operating under an active Fractional CTO maintenance contract.

4. Third-Party Services

Our website utilizes a select number of secure third-party services to function efficiently:

  • Web3Forms: Used strictly for securely transmitting your contact form submissions to our inbox.
  • Vercel & Cloudflare: Used for hosting our website and deploying edge-network architecture.

These providers have their own strict privacy policies and comply with global data protection regulations.

5. Data Security

We have put in place appropriate security measures to prevent your personal data from being accidentally lost, used, or accessed in an unauthorized way, altered, or disclosed. Access to your data is limited to employees, contractors, and agents who have a strict business need to know.

6. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this privacy policy or our privacy practices, please contact us at:

  • Email: privacy@stacknomic.io
  • Website: www.stacknomic.io
